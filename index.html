About Shobhavana

Dr Mohan Alva the chairman of Alvas Education Foundation, has made an artistic location which glorifies the nature’s beauty at Alva's Institute of Engineering and Technology (AIET) in Moodbidri named as SHOBHAVANA, which is a garden with priceless medicinal plants dedicated in the memory of Late Shobha Alva. The Herbal plantation is spread across 100 acres of land having 58 separate vanas or sub sections, and has about 2,500 varieties of medicinalplants. Shobhavana is a Certified Organic plantation and is the first of its kind medicinal garden in entire South India. There are large scale cultivation of certified organic medicinal plants which are used to produce quality herbal products by GMP certified Alva's Pharmacy which is in the same campus. There are a number of proprietary products and classical products which are manufactured traditionally and also by using modern technology..

AIET collage where education is at its peak with the remarkable knowledge provision, there situated an underrated plants park, which has abundant varieties of trees, herbs and ayurvedic plants. With a meritorious green park to explore, this special sanctuary is a heaven for nature admirers. To give a glimpse there are different parklands situated at a single area. One such a theme parkland is Ganesha Vana, which is devoted to Lord Ganesha and is blessed with his marvelous flora, that gives any person the feel of his presence. Also there is an another parkland which depicts the rich Indian cultural, ritual and astrological belief that is Navagrahas. As there are 9 planets in Indian astrology there are about their nine timed plant species are planted here to represent each with their significance and closely related qualities.

Navagraha vana

At AIET, there is a special section called Navagraha Vana that features a wide variety of plants connected to the nine celestial bodies known as the Navagrahas in Hindu astrology. These celestial bodies are said to have a profound impact on a person's life and wellbeing in Hinduism.

Ganesha vana

The adored elephant-headed god Ganesha is regarded as the god of knowledge and intellect who removes obstacles. Through the wide variety of plants that are there, the Ganesha Vana at Shobhavana AIET seeks to encapsulate the essence of Lord Ganesha's energy and symbolism.

Rashi vana

The intriguing Rashi Vana area of Shobhavana at AIET is devoted to the zodiac signs, or rashis, in Hindu astrology. Visitors can enjoy a comprehensive and immersive experience at this one-of-a-kind vana, which features a wide variety of plants and trees linked with each of the twelve zodiac signs.

Popular Trees

Sandalwood

Santalum album, also referred to as sandalwood, is a plant that has several medical uses and is highly prized for its aromatic qualities.The rich, warm, and woody scent of sandalwood helps to encourage rest, lessen stress, and quiet the mind.

Rudrakshi

The Rudraksha plant, Elaeocarpus ganitrus, is revered in Hindu mythology for its spiritual and therapeutic benefits. The Rudraksha tree's seeds, commonly known as Rudraksha beads, are frequently employed for a variety of functions, including medical ones

Noni

The Morinda citrifolia, or noni plant, is an evergreen tropical tree that is indigenous to Southeast Asia and various Pacific islands. Due to its many therapeutic qualities, it has a long history of traditional use in Polynesian, Hawaiian, and Ayurvedic medicine.

Peepal Tree

Scientific name: Ficus religiosa

Family: Moraceae

Order: Rosales

Kingdom: Plantae

The peepal tree is a kind of tree in the fig family (Moraceae), commonly known as the sacred fig or Ficus religiosa. Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism are just a few of the many civilizations and faiths that place a high value on it. Although it is native to the Indian subcontinent, peepal trees are also found in other regions of Asia. It is a big deciduous tree with heart-shaped, distinctively pointed leaves. The tree has a broad canopy and can reach a height of 30 metres.The peepal tree is revered in Hinduism and frequently linked to Lord Vishnu. It is seen to be a spiritually significant manifestation of numerous gods. Many people think that ritualistic behaviour or submerged meditation.

Arjuna tree

Scientific name: Terminalia arjuna

Family: Combretaceae

Order: Myrtales

Kingdom: Plantae

The Terminalia arjuna, also referred to as the Arjuna tree, is a species of giant deciduous tree that is indigenous to the Indian subcontinent. It is named after the legendary warrior prince Arjuna from the Hindu epic, the Mahabharata, and is a member of the Combretaceae family. In India, the Arjuna tree can be found all throughout, but it is most prevalent in the country's central and southern regions. It usually reaches a height of 20 to 27 metres and has a spreading crown with drooping branches. Smooth and grayish-white with a distinctive pattern of vertical stripes, the bark of the Arjuna tree is smooth.The therapeutic properties of the Arjuna tree have been utilised in several aspects of traditional Indian medical systems like Ayurveda. Particularly the bark is highly prized for its medicinal properties. It is thought to Arjuna bark is frequently processed into powders, extracts, or decoctions that are utilised in herbal medicines and Ayurvedic formulations. It is thought to boost cardiovascular health overall, increase blood circulation, control blood pressure, and assist strengthen the heart muscles. It is also renowned for having anti-inflammatory and antioxidant qualities.

Pomegranate

Scientific name: Punica granatum

Family: Lythraceae

Order: Myrtales

Kingdom: Plantae

Punica granatum is the scientific name for the fruit-bearing plant that produces pomegranates. It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is a tiny, deciduous shrub or tree. Although pomegranate plants are native to the area from Iran to the Himalayas in northern India, they are now grown in many other climate-friendly regions of the world. Pomegranate plants are well-known for its colourful, unusual fruit, which is normally spherical and the size of a large apple. The fruit's skin is rough and leathery and varies in colour from deep red to yellowish. The pomegranate's interior is made up of many juicy seeds called arils that are covered in a white pith. The fruit's edible section, the arils, are coveted for its sweet flavour.Pomegranate plants can survive and grow in a variety of conditions, from arid to subtropical. They love soil that drains well and full light. Pomegranate trees can take several years to mature and begin bearing fruit. They are often propagated through cuttings or grafting.

Night-flowering Jasmine

Scientific name:  Nyctanthes arbor-tristis

Family: Oleaceae

Order: Lamiales

Kingdom: Plantae

The Parijatha tree, sometimes called the Coral Jasmine or Night-flowering Jasmine, is a lovely and incredibly fragrant flowering tree.In many cultures, the Parijatha tree has great religious and cultural significance. It is regarded as a divine tree in Hindu mythology and is connected to Lord Krishna. The tree is revered as a representation of eternal love, divine beauty, and devotion. In Indian tradition, the Parijatha tree has long been regarded as sacred. It is revered and connected to a number of religious and mythical beliefs. The tree is renowned for its therapeutic qualities as well. Traditional Ayurvedic medicine uses its leaves, blossoms, and bark to cure a variety of illnesses, such as fever, arthritis, digestive disorders, and skin conditions.

Vitex Trifolia

Scientific name: Vitex trifolia

Family: Lamiaceae

Order: Lamiales

Kingdom: Plantae

Vitex trifolia is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, also known as three-leaf beggarticks or three-part beggarticks. Clusters of the plant's tiny, golden blooms are produced. The flowers are shaped like discs and are encircled by a number of bracts. Following flowering, the plant releases tiny seeds with barbed ends that make it simple for them to cling to clothing, feathers, or fur, aiding in their spread.Three-leaf Beggarticks are significant ecologically because they serve as a source of food for some insects and birds.

Leo Tree

Scientific name: Ficus racemosa

Family: Moraceae

Order: Rosales

Kingdom: Plantae

The Moraceae family of trees includes Ficus recemosa, also referred to as the Leo Tree or the Red Sandalwood Fig. Ficus recemosa is a species that is native to parts of South Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Myanmar, however it is not a commonly known or well-researched species.The Leo Tree can grow to a height of around 10 to 20 metres (33 to 66 feet), which is a moderate height. Large, glossy leaves and small, rounded fig fruits that become scarlet when ripe are what make this plant unique.

Areca

Scientific name: Areca catechu

Family: Arecaceae

Order: Arecales

Kingdom: Plantae

Areca catechu, also referred to as the areca nut plant, is a type of palm tree that is a member of the Arecaceae family. It is also frequently referred to as the supari palm or the betel nut palm. Although it is native to tropical Asia, mainly India and Malaysia, the areca nut palm is now grown in many locations throughout the world with acceptable conditions. A medium-sized tree, the areca nut palm normally reaches a height of 10 to 20 metres. It has a thin stem and fluffy, arched leaves that can grow up to 2.5 metres long on its crown. Large clusters of oval-shaped fruits, also referred to as areca nuts or betel nuts, are produced by the tree.
